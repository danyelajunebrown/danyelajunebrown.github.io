DANYELA JUNE BROWN

Danyela June Brown is a set-designer and choreographer from South Arlington, VA interested in autonomy, access, and Black/trans use-value. She has presented work at Bard College, Performance Space New York, the Hirshhorn Sculpture Garden, Dance Place, Touchstone Gallery, Rhizome DC, and the Dance Institute of Washington. She has danced for Aleta Hayes, Olaiya Olayemi, Rashad Pridgen, Hushidar Mortezaie, and the Houses of Mizrahi (2017-2019), and Kiki Houses of Old Navy (2018-2020) and Supreme (2023-Present). She has completed EMERGENYC (2020), the Learning and Public Engagement Fellowship at Dia Art Foundation (2019-2021), and the Peer Case Management Institute at Howard School of Social Work (2024). She holds a BA in African & African American Studies from Stanford and is an MFA Candidate in Sculpture at the Milton Avery Graduate School of the Arts. Danyela works as a Permanent Supportive Housing Intake Specialist for DC Department of Human Services, she served as Artistic Director of DMV Kiki Nights (2022-2025), and was the Curator of Performance for Queer Art Salon in 2024 and 2025.
