DANYELA JUNE BROWN

Danyela June Brown is a contemporary set-maker and researcher from South Arlington. A transmulatta military-brat, she tracks breaks in repressive systems by collaging, casting, computing, and making occasions. She was raised in church, and has trained with the Peridance Center Custodians, Hood By Air Museum, and in the Ballroom Scene.

Danyela holds a BA in African American Studies from Stanford University. She has presented sets at Performance Space New York, FABnyc and Sound Scene and has completed EMERGENYC (2020), the Learning and Public Engagement Fellowship at Dia Art Foundation (2019-2021) and the DHS Peer Case Management Institute at Howard University School of Social Work (2024).

Danyela has taught workshops at Dance Place, MakerspaceNYC and the Kennedy Center REACH. Currently, she serves as Artistic Director at DMV Kiki Nights, curating Ballroom Scene programming and supporting artists in DC.
